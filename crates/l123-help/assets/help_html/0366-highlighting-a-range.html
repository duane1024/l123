Highlighting a Range

Highlighting a Range -- When 1-2-3 prompts you to specify a range, you can
highlight the range.

1. Move the cell pointer to one corner of the range and press . (period)
    to anchor the cell pointer. 1-2-3 now displays a range address for the
    current cell (for example, A5..A5) in the control panel to show that
    the cell pointer is anchored.

    Some commands, such as /Copy, /Move, and /Range Format, begin with the
    cell pointer anchored in the current cell. To highlight a range that
    starts somewhere else, press ESC to unanchor the cell pointer, move
    the cell pointer to the appropriate cell, and anchor the cell pointer
    there.

    For these commands, it is often easier to highlight a range if you move
    the cell pointer to one corner of the range before you select the
    command.
