Date Formats

Date Formats -- In l123, dates are represented by values 1 (the date number
for January 1, 1900) through 73050 (the date number for December 31, 2099).
To display a value as a date, enter a date number in the worksheet
or an @function that calculates a date number, such as @DATE,
@NOW, or @TODAY. Then format the cell to display the date.

    Date Formats                      Examples for October 9, 1993
    1  DD-MMM-YY                      09-Oct-93
    2  DD-MMM                         09-Oct
    3  MMM-YY                         Oct-93
    4  Long Intn'l                    10/09/93
       MM/DD/YY (default), DD/MM/YY,
       DD.MM.YY, or YY-MM-DD
    5  Short Intn'l                   10/9
       MM/DD (default), DD/MM,
       DD.MM, or MM-DD
Use /Worksheet Global Default Other International Date to change the Long
Intn'l and Short Intn'l date formats.
